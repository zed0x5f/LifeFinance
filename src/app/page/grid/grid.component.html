The Life Insurance Settlement Market

A Life Settlement involves selling an existing life insurance policy to a third party—a person or an entity other than the company that issued the policy—for more than the policy's cash surrender value, but less than the net death benefit.

The Opportunity

Though most Life Settlement applicants are seeking relief from escalating premium costs in their latter years, more that 20% of policy owners go through the lengthy sales process, and for various reasons, do not accept a cash offer. This non-sale creates an unserved, yet motivated, market for the Superior Life premium finance product. We estimate the market to be greater than $1B in face value per year with an average of $50M in annual premium.

Life Settlements brokers will now have an additional, commercially competitive, option to offer their clients who don't feel the Life Settlement offer is acceptable, but who still want to resolve the cash flow issues of paying increased policy premiums at a time when their income is fixed.

5 Year Asset-Backed Loan:

  • Loans are backed by the life insurance policies issued by top carriers
  • Premium finance loans made based on 50% LTV of current Life Settlement market high bid
  • No further policy premium outlay for the loan period
  • Interest is accrued
  • Policy value is certain

Two Potential Outcomes

Policyholder lives to end of term, loan is paid back with interest, or policy is sold on the secondary market to satisfy the loan

Policyholder dies, loan is paid back with interest and beneficiaries collect remainder of death benefit
